Spies & Covert Ops

From BattleDawn

Jump to: navigation, search

In Battledawn, every colony and outpost can be infiltrated by a Spy planting an Agent. Spies are units, just like a normal squad, except they travel alone. To move one to a colony to infiltrate, you move it as you would a normal squad. Spies do not fight squads as normal, instead plant undercover agents in targets who can then later preform covert ops.

Note: A spy moves at the same speed as a defending unit, meaning that it can arrive faster than attacking units, which can be useful.

Spies have a one-time cost of 1 and 25 every time they're trained. When trained, they appear on your colony after the 24 ticks training time. Once a spy is seated on a target, he may plant an agent for 25. Note that agents cannot move from their target. Note that each ruler can have a maximum on 10 agents at a time.

Conducting Covert Ops

Infiltrating A Colony

Move the spy to the colony or outpost you wish to infiltrate, then plant an Agent. For each tick you leave the Agent there, it will gain an infiltration tick. The more infiltration ticks you have, the harder it is for an opponent to resist the spy attack.

Conducting A Covert Op

Each tick the planted Agent gains +1 to his infiltration points. Once the Agent reaches the amount of inflitration points needed for each specific operation, the owner of the agent can click on the infiltrated Colony/Outpost and on the big "S" to select a Covert Op and commence it.

Spies and reuse

Spies, once used to plant an Agent is free to move straight away, whether that is to plant another agent or move back to an allied colony.

Relics

Any relic cannot be spy attacked while under admin control. Once a relic is controlled by a player, spies are able to perform covert ops on the player controlled relic.

Basic Covert Ops

To perform basic covert ops and train your spy, you need to have a Spy Center built.


Spread Virus

Cost: 30 & 6
Reduces all Infantry in the target to 1 hit point.


Destroy Vehicle Fuel Supplies

Cost: 30 & 6
Reduces all Vehicle in the target by 50% of their initial hit points.


Destroy Heavy Vehicle Fuel Supplies

Cost: 40 & 8
Reduces all Tanks in the target to 50% of their initial hit points.


Boo!

Cost: 10 & 2
Sends an event to the owner of the target, revealing that he has an enemy Agent on his colony.


Advance Covert Ops

To perform advance covert ops, you need to have a Spy Academy built.


EMP Overload

Cost: 120 & 48
Removes all Energy from the target Colony (Can only be used on colonies).


Burn Oil Reservers

Cost: 75 & 48
Destroys 50% of the Oil reserves of the Colony (Can only be used on colonies).


Poison Water Supplies

Cost: 100 & 48
Kills 10% of the workers in the Colony (Can only be used on colonies).


Lockdown

Cost: 50 & 6
Prevents squads or nukes from leaving the target for 3 to 6 ticks.


Spy Protection

Spy protection or spy alert is important to avoid the possibly fatal effects of agents on yourself. It's the primary means of defending against spies, and should be maintained at all times. The amount of spy protection you have is shown as a little orange box at the button right of your colony. Your colony's spy protection does not extend to your outposts.

Adding Protection

While your master spy is on your colony, or any allied location, you will gain 1 Spy Protection per tick. Which means if you put your master spy onto your colony, after 5 ticks, you will have 5 more spy protection.

To add more protection, you exchange 50 Energy for 50 Spy Protection Points a time. You may exchange multiple times for more protection.


1. Click on your Colony.
2. Click on the actions icon.



3. In the window that appears, click "Add Spy Protection"
4. Then click "Confirm"


If this is the first purchase of Spy Protection, a dark green box with the number "50" will appear at the bottom-right of the purchaser's Colony. Additional purchases will increment this number by 50 points each time.

Whilst your spy and agent is stationed at an allied target (be it outpost, colony or even relic, belonging to you or an alliance-mate) you can place spy protection at the reduced rate of 20 for 50 protection points.

Losing Protection

For every tick that passes, 1 Spy Protection point is automatically lost. More is lost if someone attempts to carry out a covert op.

Success?

If someone tries to carry out a Covert Op on a Colony with Protection and has more infiltration points that the Colony has Protection points, the Covert Op will succeed. If the Protection points are greater than the enemy's infiltration points, the Colony's Protection points are reduced by the number of the enemy's infiltration points.

For example:

  • Colony A has had an Agent at Colony B for 7 days now (168 ticks) and therefore has 168 infiltration points.
  • Colony B has regularly topped up his/her protection and has a total of 248 spy protection points.
  • Colony A tries to conduct a covert op (any would do).
  • Results:
    • Covert Ops fails and Colony A's Agent is lost.
    • Colony B spy protection falls to 80 (248-168=80).
    • Colony B receives a message telling him that Colony A has tried to conduct a covert op.


Disbanding an Agent

If you wish to remove your agent from an outpost or colony that you have been infiltrating, you have the option to "disband" it, which is basically deleting it. You can do a covert action to make sure the agent is no longer there, but that uses energy, disbanding doesn't.


Some common reasons for disbanding agents are:

You need an agent somewhere else
Example: You need an agent at OP 15, but you already have 10 agents which means you can't plant one on OP 15 (remember, you can have a maximum of 10 agents at one point in time). In order to be able to plant an agent on OP 15, you disband an agent from another op.

You need more worker growth
Don't forget that for each agent that is planted, it adds -1 to your worker growth.


How to disband an agent?

  1. Open your "Orders" screen at the top left, and click on the "Agents" tab to the left
  2. Select the agent you wish to disband, and hit the red X disband button
