Examples of flags and halos

Every colony and outpost in Battledawn will have a certain colour halo and most will also have a flag. Flags and the colour of the halo serve 3 main purposes;

  • To distinguish between outposts/colonies belonging to hostile, neutral and friendly alliances
  • To distinguish between conquered and unconquered colonies
  • To distinguish whether a colony/outpost belongs to an alliance or not.

Meaning of Flags in Battledawn

Alliance Membership Status Flags (The top of the two rectangular flags)

  • Orange: Shows the leader of an alliance.
  • White: Shows normal members of an alliance.
  • None: Shows colonies not part of an alliance.

Conquered Status (The bottom of the two rectangular flags)

  • Yellow: Distinguishes a colony conquered by an alliance. If block solid yellow without any letters, the conquerer is not part of an alliance.
  • Blue: Distinguishes a colony conquered by an alliance that is marked friendly to your current alliance.
  • Red: Distinguishes a colony conquered by an alliance that is marked hostile to your current alliance.
  • None: Free colony.

Meaning of Halos in Battledawn

  • White: Colony or outpost that belongs to you.
  • Green: Colony or outpost is/belongs to a member of your alliance.
  • Teal: Colony or outpost that belongs to an alliance that is marked friendly to yours.
  • Red: Colony or outpost that belongs to an alliance that is marked hostile to yours and/or a colony that has conquered yours.
  • Yellow: Colony or outpost that is neutral to your alliance (all outposts/colonies are neutral if you have no alliance)
  • Dark Blue: Colony that is conquered by a member of your alliance
  • Dark Blue With Orange Star: Colony that is conquered by you
  • Purple: Colony or outpost that has had either it's alliance or owner marked specifically by you (regardless of your alliance status)

Workers & Colony Sizes

As the number of workers in your colony increases, the image for your colony changes. The more workers you have, the bigger the colony.
